Palak Paneer

As with most of my recipes, this tends to be iconoclastic, but is still satisfying. The heat is dialed in on the mild side. Make adjustments as you see fit.

Serves 2 with rice, naan, or cauliflower rice

Ingredients

  • 8 - 10 oz frozen spinach/li>
  • 28 oz. can diced tomatoes
  • 2-3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 4-5 oz queso fresco, diced
  • 1/4 cup onion, diced
  • 1 cup sour or heavy cream
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom
  • 1/2 tsp ginger
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp curry powder

Instructions

  1. Preheat a pot to medium-high heat on the stove
  2. Add the butter and melt
  3. Add the onions and spinach. Cook until tender
  4. Add the tomatoes and spices and cook until thoroughly heater through
  5. Using a blender, immersion blender, or food processor puree the ingredients until smooth
  6. Replace ingredients in the pot and adjust the heat to medium low
  7. Add queso fresco and cream. Stir until heated through (3-5 minutes). The cheese should not melt

Kale and Gruyere Pizza

This is simple pizza with more complexity in flavor than ingredient profile shows.

Serves 2-3

Ingredients

  • 1 small-medium bunch of kale, rinsed
  • 2-3 oz gruyere or other Swiss cheese, shredded
  • Fresh mozzarella thinly sliced or shredded
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 - 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • 1 serving basic dough

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 450
  2. "Wash" the kale in a bowl using 1/2 tsp of salt
  3. Roll out the dough. Using a fork, poke holes in the dough to prevent it from puffing during cooking
  4. Brush 2 tbsp of olive oil on to the dough. Sprinkle the garlic evenly and top with gruyere.
  5. Bake the crust for 2 minutes
  6. Remove from the oven and layer the kale on the crust. Sprinkle with red pepper
  7. Top uniformly with mozzarella slices
  8. Bake for an additional 8 minutes until the mozzarella begins to brown slightly
  9. Remove from oven and let sit 5 minutes before slicing to serve.
